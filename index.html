My Story

Back in High School, I was definitely not a party-all-night kind of teenager. No, I spent most of my free time reading books and practicing Visual Basic, a very simple programming language that is used to make brilliant applications such as AutoCAD. While you won't see VB or .NET much at all these days, it was a great place to start diving into functional programming with C# and C++.

At the time, I didn't quite know what I wanted to do with my life and honestly believed that I could make a living with a music degree. Over the Summer of 2017, I quickly realized that the one thing I enjoyed doing on my weekends was a huge career opportunity. I wanted to become a software engineer. I started off simple, learning Web Design with HTML, CSS and JavaScript and instantly loved it all. By the way, this website is completely hard coded, with no use of expensive third-party software.

Also, GitHub is brilliant.

Games

I've been playing video games all my life, and every now and then a specific thought pops into my head: "How did the devs do this?!" The Legend of Zelda is a great example of a game that provokes this thought. There are so many small details and core parts of the game that are intentionally implemented by developers but rarely get any attention. Even the simplest of games take a decent amount of backend skills in programming.

Video games are extremely important to me, and I can't say I'd be able to stay sane without them. For me, the greatest feeling in the world is coming home and being able to relax and play a casual simulation game after a long day of classes, or queueing up with some randoms and competing against other super-n00bs online.

Never let yourself be bored.

Technology

As a child, I was raised to believe that screens = bad and sunshine + fresh air = good. Unfortunately for me, that's correct. When I turned thirteen years old, I got a hold of my first smart device ever: the iPod Touch. I instantly fell in love with it and all of its capabilities including both gaming and a portable visual music gallery. I had ditched my Nintendo™ Wii and DS for almost a year because it was such an amazing experience.

While I'm not the biggest Apple™ fan, the company has produced some fantastic lineups of laptops and mobile phones. I haven't studied Swift, and do not plan to any time soon, but it has definitely shaped the iPhone scene in the most permanent way this past decade.

Personal Computers are becoming increasingly more popular as video games like Fortnite and PUBG take the market by storm and run Microsoft™ and Sony™ out of the gaming industry.
