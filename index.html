My Story

High school was meh. I wasn't a sociable guy. I liked my electronics and for nobody else to have them. In my junior year, I realized that I sucked at most things, but fortunately my computing knowledge was not one of them. I enrolled in a programming course later that year and learned one of my first languages: Visual Basic.

After I finished this course, I moved to C-Sharp and everything had suddenly clicked. Now that I understood one language, everything else was all just translation from thereon. All I really had to do was change semicolons to braces, syntax was generally the same, and some objects and methods would directly carry over with no change in application.

Familiarizing myself with the nomenclature and the habits of the world of software, I noticed just how much time I began spending on Stack Overflow and Stack Exchange looking for answers to problems I was too lazy to solve myself. Accepting defeat is the easy part, copy-pasting it is the harder part.

Also, thank you GitHub.
