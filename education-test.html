My Story

Back in High School, I was definitely not a party-all-night kind of teenager. No, I spent most of my free time reading books, practicing guitar and playing video games. In my junior year, I had decided that I wanted to major in Computer Science, and subsequently took a course on Visual Basic.

Perhaps one of the easiest programming languages to learn, it was a struggle for me to really adapt to backend programming; given how easy I pictured it to be. But after I finished this course, I was introduced to C-Sharp and everything had suddenly clicked. Regardless of the fact that usage of C# and VB has gone down dramatically in recent years, I'll be forever grateful for the harsh lessons they taught me along the way.

Towards the end of 2017, as I was now a Senior, I was fully hooked on coding - both frontend and backend. I would eventually develop an ever-growing habit from doing extensive research on weekends that would skyrocket me into the fantastic world of software. It was at this point that I finally knew what I wanted to become. I wanted to become a software developer. I started off nice and simple, learning Web Design with HTML, CSS3 and JavaScript and instantly loved it all.

Also, GitHub is brilliant.


Video Games

Video Games

I've been playing video games all my life, and every now and then a specific thought pops into my head: "How were they able to do this?!" The Legend of Zelda is a great example of a game that constantly provokes this question. There are so many beautifully crafted puzzles and dungeons in each release of this game's history, that I often find myself wondering if I'd ever be able to re-create something even close to what I'd experienced in-game. Maybe this isn't something that an everyday gamer would ask themselves, but it sure does bug me sometimes -- not knowing a plausible answer.

Video games are extremely important to me, and I can't say I'd be able to stay sane without them. For me, the greatest feeling in the world is coming home and being able to relax and play a casual simulation game after a long day of classes, or queueing up with some randoms and competing against a bunch of other super-n00bs online.

Never stop learning.



Technology

Technology

Electronics and Engineering have always fascinated me and I remember spending countless hours as a child just researching the best devices and electronic accessories. After playing on consoles for the majority of my childhood, Steam™ was becoming extremely popular and PC gaming became the ultimate meta. By the time I was fifteen, I had already learned how to build my own Personal Computer and permanently merged to using computers.

While I'm not the biggest Apple™ fan, the company has produced some fantastic lineups of laptops and mobile phones. I haven't studied Swift , and do not plan to any time soon, but it has definitely shaped the world of mobile phones in the most permanent way this past decade.

Personal Computers are becoming increasingly more popular as video games like Fortnite and League of Legends take the market by storm and run Microsoft™ and Sony™ out of the gaming industry.
